GPU

A GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) is a specialized hardware component designed to handle graphics and image processing tasks efficiently. It is optimized for parallel processing, meaning it can perform many calculations at the same time. GPUs are widely used in gaming, video editing, and rendering high-quality visuals. They are more powerful than CPUs for tasks involving large amounts of graphical data. Modern GPUs are also used in artificial intelligence and machine learning applications. Companies like NVIDIA and AMD manufacture some of the most popular GPUs.

ABOUT GPU

GPUs contain thousands of cores that allow them to process multiple operations simultaneously. This makes them extremely useful for high-performance tasks such as gaming, AI models, and simulations. They also support technologies like ray tracing for realistic lighting effects. With increasing demand, GPUs are becoming a key part of modern computing systems.
